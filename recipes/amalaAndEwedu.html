Amala and Ewedu

Image from this pinterest post

Amala and Ewedu, recognized as the most popular dish of Yoruba people of South-western Nigeria comprises an interesting combination of flavour and texture encompassing so much nutrient making it not far off from an overly balanced diet. Amala is a traditional Nigerian dish classified under the staple swallow category, made from yam or cassava flour. Ewedu on the other hand is its usual acquittance prepared with jute leaves imperfectly blended and served with either fish or beef stew or any other alternative.

Ingredients

  • Locust beans
  • Crayfish
  • Bouillon powder - the cubes can also be used
  • salt
  • Jute leaves - can be plucked from a farmland or bought packaged
  • Yam flour

Procedure

  1. Pick a few full sized bouquet of Jute leaves and blend with a generous amount of crayfish and locust beans together with a little bit of water to get it slurpy
  2. Turn the mixture (ewedu) into a pot and cook it on medium heat
  3. When the ewedu comes to a boil add in a pinch of salt and few ounces of bouillon powder for some extra flavour and cook on low heat until done
  4. As for your amala, bring a pot of water to a boil - it's amount depends on how many servings you want
  5. Sift enough yam flour to replace the quantity of water
  6. Stir the water quickly and continuously as you add in the yam flour little by little on medium heat
  7. Stop adding when a generously thick paste is formed and continue stirring till it's consistent enough
  8. Add water to thin it out if it's to thick
  9. Take the amala of heat and continue stirring till it's very smooth
  10. You can serve the amala and ewedu side by side with either beef or fish stew or any other meat option. It's done as you wish. Some also prefare to serve with gbegiri - a type of soup made with cooked beans
