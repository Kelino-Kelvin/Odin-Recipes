Jollof Rice

Image from this Wikipedia Page

Of course, this list of popular Nigerian foods would not be complete without the inclusion of jollof rice. Regarded as the staple of West Africa, it’s Nigeria’s go to meal for every party, celebration, event, hang out, etc. You name it, jollof rice is there! It’s enjoyed by many other countries ranging from Ghana to Sierra-Loene, but each country does have its own unique variation of the meal. For here in Nigeria, ours is known to be spicy and often highlights ingredients like peppers and crayfish.


Ingrediensts

  • 10 roma tomatoes - Medium sized
  • Bell pepper and medium sized onion - 2 each
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Groundnut oil
  • Tomato paste
  • Parboiled rice - 4cups
  • Stock chicken - about 1kg
  • Salt, curry powder and thyme
  • All purpose seasoning and seasoning cubes
  • Bay leaves

Procedure

  1. Blend your tomatoes, 1 onion and peppers smoothly. You can chop them roughly before blending to make it easier and smoother
  2. In a pot, heat half a cup of groudnut oil and add your other onion(diced) when hot.
  3. Fry till it turns golden brown and add about 6 tbsps of tomato paste to the oil. Fry it for about 3 mins
  4. Add your blended mixture whilst reserving about 2/3 cups for later. Fry the mixture for thirty mminutes making sure you stir at intervals so it doesn't burn
  5. Turn down your heat to medium and add the chicken stock. Also add half a tsp of curry powder and thyme, then 1tsp all purpose seasoning and salt to the mixture. Don't forget the cubes - about 2 will do. Boil everything for 10mins
  6. Add your parboiled rice to the pot and mix while still on heat. Add water if needed making sure the rice is leveled with the tomato mixture.
  7. Add about 5-6 bay leaves and cover the pot. Cook for 10-15mins on medium heat
  8. When the mixture is almost dried up, add the remaining tomato mixture and cover the pot. Cook till completely dried and then mix thoroughly.
    9. Your Jollof rice is done and just about ready to be eaten! 😁
