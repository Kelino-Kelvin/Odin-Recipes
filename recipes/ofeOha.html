Ofe Oha


Ofe Oha is a soup indigenous to the Igbo ethnic group in Nigeria. Itâ€™s a traditional meal made with the African rosewood plant and is usually served with fufu or pounded yam. Although being distinctively Igbo, it unsurprisingly appears in various parts due to its inviting flavor & texture and its dexterity to give so much satisfaction when eaten with the right kind of swallow.

Ingredients

  • 120grams Oha leaves. Avoid blending, Take your time to shred them with your hands
  • 2 tbps of ogiri
  • ground crayfish
  • Palm oil
  • Pepper and salt to taste
  • 7 cups water
  • A thickener of your choice
  • A meat option of your choice. About 500grams
  • 500 grams stockfish

Procedures

  1. Add meat and just enough water to cover it in a pot
  2. Add the necessary seasoning and cook for 18-20mins. Check out this page to know adequate seasonings for beef
  3. Add your stockfish and allow to cook till it's tender
  4. When tender, add dry pepper and stir and let it stay for just a litllewhile
  5. Add palm oil - about 1.5 cooking spoons and let it cook for 10mins so it incorporated with your meat While waiting, you can wash the oha leaves
  6. Add two tablespoons of your thickener and add the ogiri
  7. Allow to cook for a bit then add the oha leaves
  8. Stir your mixture to let the leaves spread. Taste for salt and add if necessary then let lit cook for 2 more minutes

    9. Take off your ofe oha from heat and serve yourself with your desired swallow. Check out this video from Zeezilicious Foods if you'd like to watch the process
